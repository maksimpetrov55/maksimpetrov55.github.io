If you are earning income from a business that is not incorporated, then most likely you are considered self-employed and will need to include your income on your personal tax return.

Even if you registered for a Master Business Licence and your business has a unique name, but not incorporated, you are considered self-employed and should include the gross revenue amounts in the "self-employed (business)" section

Taxable capital gain
?
Taxable capital gain arise from sale of stock/crypto, property, business etc.

If you purchased 10 stock x $10 = $100 + $3 transaction fee, the stock cost (ACB) is $103 and sold all stocks 6 months later for proceeds of $200 + $3 transaction fee. ACB - Proceeds = Capital Gain. $103 - $197 = $94
Taxable capital gain = 1/2 x Capital gain = $47

You will include $47 in the "taxable capital gain" section

Moving expenses
?
Expenses you can include in the "Moving expenses" section are:

Transportation and storage - parking, truck rental, mover costs, in-transit storage, insurance
Travel expenses - vehicle expenses, meals, hotel (expenses must be reasonable)
Cost of cancelling lease agreement
Incidental costs - replacing driver license, change address on legal documents, utility hook-up/disconnection

Cost of buying a new home - legal fees, land transfer tax paid (does not apply to GST/HST)

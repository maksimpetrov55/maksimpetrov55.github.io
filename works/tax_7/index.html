A small supplier is:
1) A sole proprietor, partnership or corporation AND the total amount of all revenues (before expenses) is $30,000 or less in any single calendar quarter and in the last four consecutive calendar quarters

FAQs
What if I sell in Canada and Internationally?
+
You always charge tax rate based on the place of supply. Example, if you operate out of Ontario and sell a product/service to a customer in BC, then you would charge 7% PST and 5% GST rates as per province of BC. Selling outside of Canada would mean you do not charge any GST/PST/HST.
What if my input tax credits are really low because I purchase good/services without any tax?
+
You can do a GST74 election to use the quick method of accounting and potentially save how much GST/HST you need to pay to CRA. Election can be done online through my business account or a completed form can be mailed to CRA.
Who benefits using the quick method of accounting for GST/HST?
+
Usually small business that provide services and do not have expenses that include GST/HST on them. These include IT consultants, message therapists and more.
How much can I save using the quick method of accounting for GST/HST?
+
It depends on two factors, 1) if you resell goods or provide a service and 2) the permanent establishment of your business. For more information check out our GST/HST info page.
Difference between zero rated and exempt supplies?
+
Zero rated supplies: means that you do not charge GST/HST on these supplies, but you may be eligible to claim input tax credits for the GST/HST paid on property and services acquired to provide these supplies.

The following are examples of supplies taxable at 0% (zero-rated):
1) basic groceries such as milk, bread, and vegetables;
2) agricultural products such as grain, raw wool, and dried tobacco leaves;
3) most farm livestock;
4) most fishery products such as fish for human consumption;
5) prescription drugs and drug-dispensing services;
6) certain medical devices such as hearing aids and artificial teeth;
7) feminine hygiene products;
8) exports (most goods and services for which you charge and collect the GST/HST in Canada, are zero-rated when exported); and
9) many transportation services where the origin or destination is outside Canada.

Exempt supplies: means that you do not charge the GST/HST on these supplies of property and services, and are not entitled to claim input tax credits on property and services acquired to provide these supplies. Generally, you cannot register for the GST/HST if your business provides only exempt supplies.

The following are examples of exempt supplies:
1) a sale of housing that was last used by an individual as a place of residence;
2) long-term rentals of residential accommodation (of one month or more) and residential condominium fees;
3) health, medical, and dental services performed by licensed physicians or dentists for medical reasons;
4) child care services, where the primary purpose is to provide care and supervision to children 14 years of age or under for periods of less than 24 hours per day;
5) domestic ferry services;
6) legal aid services;
7) educational services
8) music lessons;
9) services provided by financial institutions such as lending money
10) the issuance of insurance policies by an insurer

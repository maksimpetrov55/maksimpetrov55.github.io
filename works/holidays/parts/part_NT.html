Public Holiday Eligibility Criteria
+
To qualify for pay for a Public Holiday, individuals must meet all of the following:
1) An employee must have worked for the employer for 30 days within the 12 months prior to the holiday.
2) An employee must report to work on their last scheduled work day prior to the holiday and their next scheduled work day following the holiday.
3) An employee must report to work on the holiday if they are scheduled, or called to work.
Note - Part time employees are entitled to statutory holiday pay once they meet the conditions set out above.
AND
An employee on pregnancy or parental leave is not entitled to statutory holiday pay while they are on leave.

Important Notes
+
1) If a holiday falls on a non-working day or during an employee’s vacation, the employee who qualifies must receive another working day off with pay in lieu of the holiday, or if the employee agrees, a regular day’s pay for that day
2) If employee worked on the holiday, but agreed to take a day in lieu instead of being paid out, then Holiday Pay amount is deferred until the day in lieu is taken.
3) During a week with a public holiday, employees receive overtime after working 32 hours. The 32 hours does not include any hours worked on the public holiday.

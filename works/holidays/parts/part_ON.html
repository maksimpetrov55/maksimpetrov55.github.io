Public Holiday Eligibility Criteria
+
1) Fail without reasonable cause to work all of their last regularly scheduled day of work before the public holiday or all of their first regularly scheduled day of work after the public holiday (Last and First Rule)
OR
2) Fail without reasonable cause to work their entire shift on the public holiday if they agreed to or were required to work that day.
Gross Wages
?
Enter gross wages paid over the past 4 weeks before the holiday.
This may include paid vacation time or 4% vacation pay (or more) on every cheque or vacation pay in either a portion or a lump sum.
Do not include overtime pay, termination pay, severance pay, termination of assignment pay, domestic or sexual violence leave pay, or public holiday pay or premium pay earned on other public holidays.

Employee Qualify for Holiday Pay?
?
When an employee who qualifies for the day off has agreed electronically or in writing to work on a public holiday
Most employees have the right to get the public holiday off and get paid public holiday pay. However, if an employee agrees electronically or in writing to work on the public holiday, there are two options:
1) the employee is entitled to receive regular wages for all hours worked on the public holiday, plus a substitute day off work with public holiday pay;
OR
2) if the employee agrees electronically or in writing, they are entitled to public holiday pay for the public holiday plus premium pay for all hours worked on the public holiday. In this case, the employee will not be given a substitute day off.

Important Notes
+
1) The "last regularly scheduled day of work before the public holiday" and the "first regularly scheduled day of work after the public holiday" do not have to be the days right before and right after the holiday.
2) When a public holiday falls on an employee's non-working day or during an employee's vacation
a) Employee can receive a substitute holiday off with public holiday pay;
OR
b) public holiday pay"
3) If an employee has agreed electronically or in writing to work on the public holiday but does not do so – and does not have reasonable cause for not having done so – the employee has no right to public holiday pay or to a substitute day off with pay.
